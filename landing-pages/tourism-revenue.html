Your Website Should Be Too.

Tennessee welcomed 147 million visitors and $31.7 billion in tourism spending in 2024 — and East Tennessee led the charge. Those visitors are searching for restaurants, shops, guides, and services on their phones right now. Can they find your business?

If you don't have a website — or if your only online presence is a Facebook page — millions of visitors are driving through your area, Googling exactly what you offer, and finding your competitor instead. Here's what you need to know.

The Revenue Flowing Through

$87 Million Spent Per Day in Tennessee

Tourism is Tennessee's second-largest industry. In 2024, it broke records for the fourth year in a row — and East Tennessee is where the growth is happening.

Tennessee Statewide — 2024

$31.7 Billion

In direct visitor spending — a 3.3% increase over 2023. This generated $3.3 billion in state and local tax revenue, saving each Tennessee household $1,170 per year. Tourism supports 194,820 jobs — one in every 24 across the state.

Sevier County

$3.9 Billion

Gatlinburg, Pigeon Forge, and Sevierville. Gateway to the Great Smoky Mountains — the most visited national park in America with 12.2 million visitors in 2024.

Knox County

$2 Billion

Knoxville's sports, music, food scene, and UT football draw visitors year-round. "Football season is like our gravy for the whole year," said Visit Knoxville's Kim Bumpas.

Blount County

$600+ Million

Maryville, Townsend, and the "peaceful side of the Smokies." A 6% increase over 2023 — and growing faster than the state average.

Anderson County

Growing

Oak Ridge, Clinton, Rocky Top, and Norris — home to historic sites, Norris Dam, and a growing tourism strategy. Every visitor is a potential customer for local businesses.

Data from TN Dept. of Tourist Development, Tourism Economics, and National Park Service — 2024 reports. Tennessee is the 2nd fastest-growing state in visitor spending over the last 7 years.

How Visitors Actually Find You

They're Not Asking Locals. They're Asking Google.

When 12 million visitors come through the Smokies, they don't know which restaurant is good or which mechanic is nearby. They pull out their phone and search. If your business doesn't show up, you don't exist to them.

62% Use Google to Find Restaurants

Before choosing where to eat, most visitors search Google first — not Yelp, not TripAdvisor, not social media. "Food near me" searches are up 99% year-over-year.

85% Use Phones to Book Activities

From restaurant reservations to sightseeing tours, tourists are booking on mobile. And 48% of experience bookings happen after arriving at the destination.

"Near Me" Searches Up 900%

Hyperlocal searches — "coffee near me," "things to do near me" — have exploded. Mobile searches for activities near me have seen a 6x increase in recent years.

80% of Local Searches Lead to a Visit

When someone searches for a physical business with local intent, 4 out of 5 actually go there. These aren't casual browsers — they're ready to spend money.

72% Visit Within 5 Miles

Local searchers tend to visit businesses close to where they're searching from. For tourists driving through East Tennessee, that means your town.

83% Check Reviews Before Deciding

Visitors don't just search — they evaluate. Most consumers read Google reviews before choosing a business. No reviews or no listing means no trust.

Your Free Secret Weapon

Google Business Profile: The Tool You Might Not Be Using

Google Business Profile is free. It puts your business on Google Maps and in local search results. And for tourism-area businesses, it's one of the most powerful things you can do — yet many small businesses either haven't claimed theirs or haven't filled it out.

Why This Matters

Verified Profiles Are 2.7x More Likely to Be Trusted

A complete Google Business Profile with accurate info, photos, and reviews signals to visitors that your business is real, active, and worth their time. Without one, you're invisible in the exact place tourists are looking.
