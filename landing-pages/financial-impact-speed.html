The Real Cost

Every Second Costs You Customers

Researchers studied billions of real website visits and found the same thing every time — the longer your site takes to load, the more people leave without ever seeing what you offer.

What happens as your site gets slower:

Just 0.1 seconds slower 7% fewer customers
1 second slower 22% fewer customers
2 seconds slower 37% fewer customers
3+ seconds Over half leave immediately

Think about what that means for your business. If 10 people a day find your site but leave because it's slow, that's 3,650 potential customers a year who never saw your hours, your services, or your phone number.

What Slow Looks Like

This Is Happening to Real Businesses

If the biggest companies on earth lose money from slow websites, imagine what it's doing to a local business that can't afford to lose a single customer.

Local Shops
A 3-second site loses half its visitors

Someone searches "plumber near me" and clicks your site. If it doesn't load fast, they hit the back button and call your competitor. You never even knew they were there.

Mobile Users
60%+ of your traffic is on a phone

Mobile connections are slower than desktop. If your site isn't built for speed on a phone, you're losing the majority of your audience before they even see your homepage.

Google Rankings
Slow sites get buried in search results

Google uses page speed as a ranking factor. A slow site doesn't just lose impatient visitors — it also shows up lower in search results, meaning fewer people find you in the first place.
