The Hidden Costs

What You're Missing Without a Website

Social media is great for engagement and community. But when it's your only online presence, you're giving up more than you realize.

You Don't Show Up on Google

46% of all Google searches are local. When someone searches "plumber in Oak Ridge" or "bakery near me," your Facebook page is almost never what appears. A website is.

You're Building on Rented Land

You don't own your Facebook page — Meta does. They control who sees your posts, what features you have, and whether your account stays active. A website is yours.

You Look Less Credible

75% of consumers judge a business's credibility by its website. A social-media-only business can appear less established or trustworthy to potential customers.

You're Paying to Be Seen

99% of Meta's revenue comes from advertising. The low organic reach isn't a bug — it's the business model. They want you to pay for every set of eyeballs.

You Don't Own Your Audience

Your followers aren't yours. If your account gets hacked, suspended, or if the platform shuts down a feature, you could lose your entire customer base overnight.

Your Content Disappears

A Facebook post has a lifespan of hours. A blog post on your website can drive traffic for years. Social media is a feed — a website is a foundation.

When the Platform Goes Dark

What Happens When Facebook Goes Down?

If your only online presence is social media, a platform outage means your business effectively ceases to exist online. This isn't hypothetical — it's happened multiple times in the last few years.

  1. October 2021

    Facebook, Instagram & WhatsApp Down for 6 Hours

    A configuration error took all Meta services offline globally. Small businesses reported losing hundreds to thousands of dollars in sales. One company saw revenue drop 70% in a single day. Meta itself lost an estimated $60–100 million in ad revenue.

  2. March 2024

    Meta Apps Down for 2+ Hours on Super Tuesday

    Facebook, Instagram, Threads, WhatsApp, and Messenger all went offline. Over 100,000 users reported issues within the first 10 minutes. Businesses couldn't respond to customers, run ads, or process orders.

  3. December 2024

    Another Major Meta Outage — Hours of Downtime

    A "technical issue" during routine maintenance knocked services offline for several hours. 40% of small business owners reported a decline in sales. Over 100,000 outage reports on Downdetector at peak.

Meanwhile, during every one of these outages

Your website stays online.

Your website doesn't depend on Meta's servers, algorithms, or maintenance schedules. When social media goes dark, your website keeps working — showing up on Google, answering customer questions, and keeping your business open.

Better Together

Use Both — But Build on Ground You Own

I'm not saying ditch social media. I'm saying don't build your entire business on it. When you have a website and social media working together, each one makes the other stronger.

Website = Home Base

Your website is the central hub where customers find your hours, services, location, and contact info. It's there 24/7, indexed by Google, and owned by you.

Social = The Megaphone

Social media is how you share personality, connect with your community, and drive traffic back to your website — where visitors can actually become customers.

Google Finds Your Website

When someone searches for what you offer, Google shows websites — not Facebook posts. 40% of small businesses with websites say SEO is their number one source of leads.

Credibility from Day One

Having your own domain — yourbusiness.com — signals that you're professional and here to stay. Customers are 2.7x more likely to trust a business with a complete online profile.

Collect Leads You Own

With a website, you can build an email list — an audience you own and can contact directly, without paying an algorithm for permission to reach them.

Even a One-Pager Works

You don't need a massive website. A clean, well-built single page with your info, services, and contact details is enough to put you on the map and start showing up in search.
