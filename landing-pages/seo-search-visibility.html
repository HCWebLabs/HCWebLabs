SEO in Plain English

What SEO Actually Means for Your Business

SEO stands for Search Engine Optimization — not mysterious tech jargon. It's simply making sure the right people can find your business when they're looking for exactly what you offer. That means building your website the right way so Google understands what you do, where you are, and who you serve.

Clean, Semantic Code

Proper HTML structure with headings, labels, and landmarks tells search engines exactly what your page is about. Messy code confuses crawlers and buries your content.

Fast Load Times

Google uses page speed as a ranking factor. Sites that load in under 2.5 seconds rank higher. 53% of mobile users leave if a page takes more than 3 seconds.

Mobile-First Design

Google crawls and indexes your mobile site first. Over 60% of all searches happen on phones. If your site doesn't work on mobile, Google notices — and ranks you lower.

Local Signals

Your business name, address, phone number, service areas, and Google Business Profile all tell search engines where you operate and who should find you.

Relevant Content

Pages that clearly describe your services, answer customer questions, and use natural language rank better than thin, vague pages with no real information.

Accessibility

Accessible websites — with proper alt text, labels, and keyboard navigation — perform better in search results because the same things that help people also help crawlers.
