The Myths vs. The Reality

What Business Owners Tell Themselves — and What the Data Says

The reasons small business owners give for not having a website? Every single one has a data-backed answer. Let's walk through them.

Myth

"My business is too small to need a website."

Reality

82% of small businesses operate without employees. Half start from home. These are exactly the businesses that benefit most — because a website makes a one-person operation look established, professional, and available 24/7. Small businesses with websites grow twice as fast as those without.

Myth

"I can just use social media instead."

Reality

21% of small businesses rely solely on social media — but 84% of consumers say a website makes a business more credible than a social media page alone. 92% prefer getting information from a website over social media. You don't own your Facebook page. The algorithm decides who sees your content. A website is yours.

Myth

"A website is too expensive for what I do."

Reality

26% cite cost as the reason they skip a website. But businesses without websites lose more in missed customers than a site costs to build. 62% of consumers will ignore a business with no web presence. 31% have decided against buying from a business specifically because it lacked a website. A professional site pays for itself.

Myth

"A website isn't relevant to my industry."

Reality

27% believe this — but 81% of consumers research businesses online before making a purchase. 85% use the internet to find local businesses. Whether you're a carpenter, a baker, a tutor, or an accountant, your customers are already searching. The question is whether they're finding you or your competitor.

Myth

"I don't have the technical skills."

Reality

15% say they don't know how to build or maintain a website. That's exactly what a professional web developer is for. You don't need to know how to build it — you just need to know it matters. And 97% of small business owners who have a website would recommend it to others.

Size Doesn't Build Trust — Presence Does

Your Customers Don't Care How Big You Are. They Care That You Show Up.

Nobody googles a business hoping to find out how many employees they have. They want to know: Can I trust you? What do you offer? How do I reach you? A website answers all three before they ever pick up the phone.

A one-person home-based business with a clean, professional website looks exactly as legitimate as a ten-person office downtown. On the internet, nobody knows you're working from your kitchen table — and that's a superpower.

What It Actually Does for You

A Website Doesn't Just Exist. It Works.

For a small or home-based business, a website isn't about looking fancy. It's about doing the things you don't have time to do yourself — around the clock, every single day.

 It Answers Questions While You Sleep

Your hours, your services, your pricing, your location or service area, your contact info — all available at 2 a.m. on a Tuesday. No phone calls, no DMs, no "let me get back to you." A website is your most patient employee.

 It Makes You Findable

85% of consumers use the internet to find local businesses. If you don't have a website, you're invisible to the largest search engine on earth. Social media pages rank poorly in Google compared to a dedicated website with proper SEO.

 It Builds Instant Credibility

A professional website with your own domain and business email (you@yourbusiness.com, not a Gmail address) instantly signals that you're serious, established, and worth doing business with. 75% of consumers judge your credibility by your site's design.

 It Converts Referrals into Customers

Someone hears about you from a friend. What do they do next? They Google you. If they find a professional website, they call. If they find nothing — or just a sparse Facebook page — 30% won't even consider buying from you.

 It Gives You Control

Social media platforms change their rules constantly. Algorithms shift. Accounts get flagged. A website is your digital property — you control the message, the design, the data, and the experience. Nobody can change the algorithm on your website.

Social Media Alone Isn't Enough

A Facebook Page Is Not a Website

Social media is great for engagement. But it was never designed to replace a website. 21% of small businesses rely on it exclusively — and they're leaving credibility, search visibility, and customers on the table.

 Social Media Only

  • Algorithms decide who sees your posts
  • Limited design and branding control
  • No professional email domain
  • Poor Google search ranking
  • Limited to platform's structure and rules
  • Can be shut down or shadowbanned overnight

 Your Own Website

  • You own it — it's your digital property
  • Available to everyone who searches for you
  • Full control over brand and design
  • Professional you@yourbusiness.com email
  • Built for Google — SEO brings free traffic
  • Unlimited pages, content, and features
  • No algorithm, no shadowban, no surprises

The best strategy isn't either/or — it's both. Use social media to engage and connect. Use your website as your home base: the place every post, every referral, and every Google search leads back to. 92% of consumers prefer getting their information from a website over a social media page.
