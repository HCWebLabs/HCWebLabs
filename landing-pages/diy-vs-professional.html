I Started with Builders, Too

Website builders are genuinely great tools. They're how a lot of us got started — including us. But there's a point where a growing business needs more than a template can give it.

This page isn't about putting down DIY platforms or the people who use them. It's about helping you see where the gaps show up — and what changes when a professional builds your site from scratch.

Let's Talk About Your Site See the Full Picture
Credit Where It's Due

What Builders Do Well

I'm not here to bash website builders — they serve a real purpose and do some things genuinely well. If you're just getting started, they can be exactly what you need.

Fast to Launch

You can have something online in a weekend. For a personal project, a quick landing page, or testing an idea — that speed is valuable.

Low Entry Cost

Plans start at just a few dollars a month. When you're bootstrapping, that matters — and there's no shame in starting small.

Great for Learning

Dragging blocks around teaches you what a website needs: navigation, layout, calls to action. That experience is real, and it matters when you're ready for more.

Perfect for Personal Projects

A portfolio, a hobby blog, a family reunion page — templates handle these beautifully. Not everything needs custom code.

About 73% of small businesses in the U.S. now have a website — many of them built with these tools. That's a good thing. The question is whether the tool still fits as your business grows.

The Things Nobody Mentions

Where the Gaps Show Up

These aren't reasons to feel bad about using a builder. They're just the things most business owners discover six months in — when the limitations start costing real time and real money.

The Price Goes Up

That $3/month intro rate? It often jumps 2–3× at renewal. A plan that cost $36 the first year can quietly become $120+ once the promotional period ends — and the features you actually need are usually on the higher-tier plans.

The Add-Ons Add Up

Need email marketing? That's $13+/month. Premium templates? $30–$200. Apps for booking, payments, or analytics? Each one is another $5–$50/month. Transaction fees take another 1.5–3% off every sale. It adds up faster than you'd expect.

Everyone Looks the Same

The most popular templates have been downloaded thousands of times. Your site might look polished — but so does your competitor's, in the exact same layout with the exact same fonts. Standing out becomes very difficult.

Performance Suffers

Templates come loaded with code for features you'll never use. That bloat slows your site down, hurts your search rankings, and drives away the 53% of mobile visitors who leave if a page takes more than 3 seconds to load.

Accessibility Gets Missed

A study of websites built on the leading builder platform found that 71% had insufficient color contrast, 58% had broken heading structure, and 55% had images without proper descriptions. These aren't obscure issues — they lock out real customers.

You Don't Own Your Site

Your content lives on their servers, inside their system. If you want to leave, you often can't take your design with you. Some platforms charge for data export. Some don't offer it at all. Your business depends on their decisions.

Look Under the Hood

What Bloat Actually Looks Like

A website can look great on the surface and still be a mess underneath. Here's what's really going on behind the scenes — and why it matters for your search rankings, load speed, and accessibility.

What Builders Generate

  • Dozens of unused CSS and JavaScript files loaded on every single page — even ones that don't need them.
  • Auto-generated class names like .x7hK2_wrapper-inner that no search engine or human can read.
  • Images uploaded at full resolution with no compression, no proper sizing, and no descriptive alt text.
  • Generic or missing meta descriptions that tell Google nothing useful about your business.
  • Heading tags chosen for how they look instead of what they mean — breaking the structure search engines rely on.
  • Third-party tracking scripts and plugin dependencies stacking up with every add-on.

What Hand-Coded Looks Like

  • Only the code that page actually needs — nothing extra, nothing wasted. Every line has a purpose.
  • Clean, semantic HTML that search engines and screen readers understand at first glance.
  • Every image optimized, compressed, properly sized, and described with hand-written alt text tailored to its context.
  • Hand-written metadata crafted for each page's specific content, audience, and search intent.
  • A logical heading hierarchy that tells Google exactly what your page is about and how it's organized.
  • Zero dependency on third-party platforms — your site stands on its own, fast and clean.

The difference isn't visible to your visitors — but it's everything to Google. Clean code ranks. Bloated code doesn't.
